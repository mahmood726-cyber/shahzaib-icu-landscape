Dear Editor,

We submit the manuscript **"ICU Hemodynamic Trial Landscape: A Living Scoping Review of Registered Randomized Trials with Hemodynamic and Related Outcomes"** for consideration in *PLOS ONE*.

**Rationale:** Despite hemodynamic monitoring being central to critical care, no comprehensive mapping exists of the trial landscape for hemodynamic interventions. We address this gap using a living scoping review methodology.

**Approach:** We systematically searched ClinicalTrials.gov (22,054 studies) and PubMed (5,971 records) using 35 hemodynamic outcome categories, identifying 27,498 ICU-related randomized trials spanning 148 countries. A real-time dashboard enables continuous monitoring.

**Key findings:**
- 11,960 trials (43.5%) report hemodynamic outcomes
- Only 7.3% have both hemodynamic endpoints and placebo/sham comparators
- Trial density is concentrated in cardiology and anesthesia; nephrology and trauma are underrepresented
- Temporal analysis shows accelerating trial registration since 2005

**PLOS ONE fit:** This large-scale mapping study with an open-access dashboard and reproducible pipeline aligns with PLOS ONE's emphasis on rigorous methodology and open data. The dataset and code will be deposited on Zenodo.

The manuscript follows PRISMA-ScR reporting guidelines. All authors approve the submission. No competing interests exist.
